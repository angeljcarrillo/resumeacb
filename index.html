About Me

Angel Carrillo-Bermejo is a PhD Machine Learning Engineer, recently graduated from the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM). He worked on the thesis of a dissimilarity measure by codes chains in open curves, establishing mathematical bases and potential applications on medical singals and images. He has collaborated in works such as search for patterns for the detection of anomalies in ocular blood vessels in premature and diabetic patients. He has focused on the development of machine learning algorithms and deep neural network architectures for the detection of patterns in brain tumors, signals in pathologies such as Schizophrenia, Alzheimer's.

"I understand how a simple impulse can be the difference for a great change. I enjoy playing sports outdoors, as well as programming different architectures of deep learning with a cup of coffee".

Research interest

Deep neural networks are now the state-of-the-art machine learning models across a variety of areas, from image analysis to natural language processing, and widely deployed in academia and industry. These developments have a huge potential for medical imaging technology, medical data analysis, medical diagnostics and healthcare in general, slowly being realized.

Research Projects

Building a detection model using a convolutional neural network in Tensorflow & Keras. Used a brain MRI images data founded on Brats2020.

Dissimilarity between simple curves. Using the slope chain code, a curve is assigned to an invariant representation under translation, rotation, and scale. In order to calculate the difference between two simple curves, it is proposed to generate sub-chains of the concave and convex changes along both curves.
